CHAPTER TWO: BE A MIRROR

In the second chapter of Never Split the Difference, Chris Voss discusses the importance of using "mirroring" as a negotiation technique. Mirroring involves repeating back the other person's words or phrases in a way that demonstrates understanding and empathy.

Here are some key lessons from this chapter summarized by ChatGPT:

  1. Mirroring helps build rapport: By repeating back the other person's words, you can create a sense of understanding and connection, which can help build trust and establish a positive negotiating dynamic.
  2. Mirroring can diffuse tension: By acknowledging and reflecting the other person's emotions, you can help diffuse tension and create a more productive negotiating environment.
  3. Use mirroring to gather information: Asking open-ended questions and mirroring the other person's responses can help you gather more information and better understand their perspective.
  4. Be careful not to overuse mirroring: While mirroring can be a useful technique, it's important not to overuse it or it can come across as insincere or manipulative.
  5. Practice mirroring in different contexts: Mirroring can be a valuable tool in a variety of negotiation situations, so it's important to practice using it in different contexts to become comfortable and proficient with the technique.
